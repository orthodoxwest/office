[paschal-psalm-antiphon]
# SOURCE: divinum-officium (tempore paschali: psalms are sung under a single Alleluia antiphon, said before the first psalm and after the last) — check against diurnal
Alleluia, * alleluia, alleluia.

[opening-versicle]
V. O God, ✠ make speed to save me.
R. O Lord, make haste to help me.

Glory be to the Father, and to the Son, and to the Holy Ghost;
as it was in the beginning, is now, and ever shall be, world without end. Amen.

[pre-collect-versicles]
V. O Lord, hear my prayer.
R. And let our cry come unto thee.

Let us pray.

[daily-blessing]
V. The Lord be with you.
R. And with thy spirit.
V. Let us bless the Lord.
R. Thanks be to God.

[faithful-departed]
V. May the souls of the faithful, through the mercy of God, rest in peace.
R. Amen.

[our-father-partly-secret-rubric]
# SOURCE: General Rubrics of the Monastic Breviary, XXXV.1-2;
# 03. Terce Through the Week Alex edit 11-24 v.01.docx, p. 5
The Our Father is said secretly until And lead us not into temptation, which is said aloud; the response But deliver us from evil is also said aloud.

[closing-our-father]
Our Father is said secretly.

[closing-peace]
V. The Lord give us his peace.
R. And life everlasting. Amen.

[divine-help]
V. May the divine help remain with us always.
R. And with our absent brethren. Amen.

[commemoration-antiphon]
Pray for us, O holy N., * that we may be made worthy of the promises of Christ.

[commemoration-versicle]
V. The Lord hath chosen him for himself.
R. As his treasure for ever.

[commemoration-collect]
Grant, O Lord, we beseech thee, the prayers of thy holy servant, that we who
cannot by our own merits attain to thy grace may be helped by his intercession.
Through our Lord Jesus Christ thy Son, who with thee and the Holy Spirit liveth
and reigneth, God, for ever and ever.
R. Amen.
