[hymn-sunday]
# The Winter Hymn, said from the Octave of Epiphany
# through Quinquagesima, and from the Sunday nearest
# the first day of October until Advent
Aeterne rerum conditor

O Framer of the earth and sky,
Ruler of all things high and low,
Who, clothing time with mystery,
Dost make the seasons ebb and flow;

Herald of day, the bird doth raise
His voice in strains of varied tone,
And, breaking through the midnight haze,
Doth make the path of night-time known.

At this the morning star breaks forth
And leaves the darkness far behind,
And all the wandering hosts of night
Yield to the Morningstar their kind.

At this the sailor takes new heart,
The surging billows quit their roar:
At this the rock of Peter's tears
Dissolves in weeping o'er and o'er.

Arise, arise, then, slumbering souls;
Arise; the new day fills the sky:
The star of stars is mounting high,
Who dares may win the starry prize.

Jesu, look back on us who fall;
Thy glance can error's power restrain:
We weep, but tears are naught at all,
Unless thou melt the flinty chain.

Thou Light of lights, thy light restore;
Sloth from the mind of faith repel:
Thou First of voices, go before
Our earliest voices as they swell. Amen.

[hymn-sunday-summer]
# The Summer Hymn — said from the II Sunday after Trinity until the Sunday
# nearest the first of October (see internal/office/hymn.go).
# TODO(diurnal): verify translation against the printed diurnal.
Ecce iam noctis tenuatur umbra

Lo! the dim shadows of the night are waning;
Radiantly glowing, dawn of day returneth;
Fervent in spirit, to the mighty Father
Pray we devoutly.

So shall our Maker, of his great compassion,
Banish all sickness, kindly health bestowing;
And may he grant us, of a Father's goodness,
Mansions in heaven.

This he vouchsafe us, God for ever blessèd,
Father eternal, Son, and Holy Spirit,
Whose is the glory which through all creation
Ever resoundeth. Amen.

[hymn-monday]
Splendor paternae gloriae

O Splendour of God's glory bright,
O thou that bringest light from light,
O Light of light, light's living spring,
O Day, all days illumining;

O thou true Sun, on us thy glance
Let fall in royal radiance;
The Spirit's sanctifying beam
Upon our earthly senses stream.

The Father too our prayers implore,
Father of glory evermore,
The Father of all grace and might,
To banish sin from our delight.

To guide whate'er we nobly do,
With love all envy to subdue,
To make ill-fortune turn to fair,
And give us grace our wrongs to bear.

Our mind be in his keeping placed,
Our body true to him and chaste,
Where only faith her fire shall feed,
To burn the tares of Satan's seed.

And Christ to us for food shall be,
From him our drink that welleth free,
The Spirit's wine, that maketh whole,
And, mocking not, exalts the soul.

Joyful be our day's return,
As with the dawn our Faith shall burn,
Like noon be our believing bright,
Our souls unshadowed by the night. Amen.

[hymn-tuesday]
Nox et tenebrae et nubila

Ye clouds and darkness, hosts of night,
That breed confusion and affright,
Begone! o'erhead the dawn shines clear,
The light breaks in and Christ is here.

Earth's gloom flees broken and dispersed,
By the sun's piercing shafts coerced:
Such is the light that must efface
All inward darkness from his face.

The swooning mind's delirious dreams
Of fond desires, its wanton gleams
Of lust, are quenched by purity
In those that long thy face to see.

Gird us with chastity, O Lord,
O bid the light to be restored,
No lie to dim us, and no snare
Of guile to make us less than fair.

So, as the day runs out its course,
And brings the evening's sobering force,
Life's glories by renouncing well,
We keep a pure life's citadel. Amen.

[hymn-wednesday]
Rerum creator optime

Best Maker of the heavenly dome,
Who rulest day and night at home,
And givest time its shifting ways
In change of nights and change of days,

Hear us, and let thy light impart
Its brightness to the weary heart,
That we may never yield to sin
Though foes assail us from within.

Grant this, dread Father, throned on high,
To whom all angels ceaseless cry;
Whom Son and Spirit, too, proclaim,
And earth and sea and sky the same. Amen.

[hymn-thursday]
Lux ecce surgit aurea

Lo! the golden sun awakes,
Roused from slumber night forsakes;
Prayer arise, and as we pray,
Let all evil flee away.

All the tongue's excess control;
Guard the portals of the soul;
Let the eye no wandering take,
But virtue's watch for virtue's sake.

Let the inner man be clean;
Naught impure of heart be seen;
Pride of body, pride of will,
O let neither work us ill.

Whatso'er is hid shall shine
When the day's all-searching line
Through each heart its light shall throw;
Nothing then will lie below.

Grant us, Father, morning light;
Grant the Son to match the height
Of the Father; Spirit too
With them grant us to pursue. Amen.

[hymn-friday]
Ales diei nuntius

The bird, that is the herald of the day,
Proclaims that light is nigh;
And Christ arousing all that slumbering lay,
Doth call the souls of men to draw them nigh.

Stir up the sluggard heart, cries he aloud;
Break off the chain of sleep;
Be sober, chaste, and upright, and be bowed
In prayer, ye faithful ones who vigil keep.

Jesu, look back on us who fall;
Thy glance can error's power restrain:
We weep, but tears are naught at all,
Unless thou melt the flinty chain.

Thou Light of lights, thy light restore;
Sloth from the mind of faith repel;
Thou First of voices, go before
Our earliest voices as they swell. Amen.

[hymn-saturday]
Aurora iam spargit polum

Now the purple dawn of day
Spreads o'er heaven its glowing ray;
Come, with voices clear and strong,
Sing the morning's grateful song.

With one accord, your voices raise
To the Lord of life and praise;
In the dawn let hymns resound,
For his mercies so abound.

Night hath passed, and day holds sway;
Cheerful light puts gloom away;
Christ who guides us safe shall be,
He the light that all may see.

Hail to thee, O Christ, the King!
Praises evermore we bring;
Thee and Father, Spirit blest,
One great God whom all confessed. Amen.
