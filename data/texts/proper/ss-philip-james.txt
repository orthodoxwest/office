[psalm-antiphon-1]

[psalm-antiphon-2]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Lauds/Vespers books, pp. 449, 363, 372 (Saints Philip and James)
Philip, * he that hath seen me hath seen my Father also, alleluia.

[psalm-antiphon-3]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Lauds/Vespers books, pp. 450, 364, 373 (Saints Philip and James)
Have I been * so long time with you, and yet hast thou not known me, Philip? he that hath seen me hath seen my Father also, alleluia.

[psalm-antiphon-4]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Lauds book, p. 451 (Saints Philip and James)

[commemoration-antiphon]
@use proper/ss-philip-james/psalm-antiphon-1

[collect]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Lauds/Vespers books, pp. 457, 370, 379 (Saints Philip and James)
O God, who makest us glad by the yearly solemnity of thine Apostles Philip and James: grant, we beseech thee, that we who rejoice in their merits may be taught by their example.

[benedictus-antiphon]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Lauds book, p. 456 (Saints Philip and James)
I am the way, * the truth, and the life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by me, alleluia.

[magnificat-antiphon-first]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Vespers book, p. 369 (I Vespers for Saints Philip and James)
Let not your heart * be troubled, neither let it be afraid; ye believe in God, believe also in me: in my Father's house are many mansions, alleluia, alleluia.

R. God hath chosen you to him to be his inheritance, alleluia.
