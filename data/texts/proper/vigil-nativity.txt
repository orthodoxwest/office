[psalm-antiphon-1]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Lauds book, p. 49 (Vigil of the Nativity)
O Judah and Jerusalem, * be ye not dismayed: tomorrow go ye forth, and the Lord himself will be with you, alleluia.

[psalm-antiphon-2]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Lauds book, p. 50 (Vigil of the Nativity)
Ye shall know this day * that the Lord will come; and in the morning ye shall see his glory.

[psalm-antiphon-3]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Lauds book, p. 50 (Vigil of the Nativity)
Tomorrow * the iniquity of the earth shall be blotted out: and the Saviour of the world shall reign over us.

[psalm-antiphon-4]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Lauds book, p. 51 (Vigil of the Nativity)
The Lord will come, * go ye out to meet him, saying: Great is his dominion, and of his kingdom there shall be no end: the mighty God, the Governor, the Prince of peace, alleluia.

[psalm-antiphon-5]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Lauds book, p. 53 (Vigil of the Nativity)
Tomorrow * ye shall have salvation, saith the Lord God of hosts.

[benedictus-antiphon]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Lauds book, p. 57 (Vigil of the Nativity)
The Saviour * of the world shall arise as the sun: and shall come down into the Virgin's womb, as the showers upon the grass, alleluia.

[collect]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Lauds book, p. 58 (Vigil of the Nativity)
O God, who makest us glad with the yearly expectation of the birth of thine only Son Jesus Christ: grant that as we joyfully receive him for our Redeemer, so we may with sure confidence behold him when he shall come to be our Judge.
