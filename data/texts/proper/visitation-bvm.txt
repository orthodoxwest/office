[psalm-antiphon-1]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Lauds/Vespers books, pp. 492, 426 (Visitation BVM)
Mary arose, * and went into the hill country with haste, into a city of Judah.

[psalm-antiphon-2]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Lauds/Vespers books, pp. 493, 427 (Visitation BVM)
And Mary entered * into the house of Zacharias and saluted Elisabeth.

[psalm-antiphon-3]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Lauds/Vespers books, pp. 493, 428 (Visitation BVM)
When Elisabeth heard * the salutation of Mary, the babe leaped in her womb, and she was filled with the Holy Ghost, alleluia.

[psalm-antiphon-4]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Lauds book, p. 494 (Visitation BVM)
Blessed art thou * among women, and blessed is the fruit of thy womb.

[psalm-antiphon-5]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Lauds book, p. 496 (Visitation BVM)
For lo, as soon * as the voice of thy salutation sounded in mine ears, the babe leaped in my womb for joy, alleluia.

[psalm-antiphon-4-vespers]
@use proper/visitation-bvm/psalm-antiphon-5

[benedictus-antiphon]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Lauds book, p. 499 (Visitation BVM)
When Elisabeth * heard the salutation of Mary, she spake out with a loud voice, and said: Whence is this to me, that the mother of my Lord should come to me? alleluia.

[magnificat-antiphon]
# SOURCE: draft archdiocesan Vespers book, p. 432 (Visitation BVM)
Blessed art thou, * O Mary, for thou hast believed: and there shall be a performance in thee of those things which were told thee from the Lord, alleluia.

[commemoration-versicle]
@use shared/blessed-virgin/versicle-blessed-art-thou

[collect]
Grant, O Lord, we beseech thee, unto all thy servants, the gift of thine heavenly grace, that even as the Blessed Virgin being made a Mother hath been unto them the first step unto their salvation, so the godly and solemn memorial of her Visitation may be the bringer of an increase of peace.
